IROS 2026: Under Review

Anatomy-Specific Guidewire Personalization
for Neuroendovascular Navigation

Institution
Worcester Polytechnic Institute
Program
M.S. Robotics Engineering
Timeline
2025 to Present
Status
In Progress · IROS 2026 Submitted

Current endovascular tools use one-size-fits-all designs despite wide anatomical variation in the internal carotid artery. This work builds a patient-specific optimization pipeline, from medical image segmentation to physics simulation to hardware validation, that personalizes guidewire tip geometry to individual ICA anatomy, improving navigation success and reducing contact forces.

SOFA Framework BeamAdapter Genetic Algorithm TOF-MRA VMTK Fluoroscopy Benchtop Robotics ROS2 Python
Key Contributions
  • 100-patient anatomy benchmark from open-source TOF-MRA data
  • Physics simulation in SOFA with BeamAdapter for catheter + guidewire in vascular geometry
  • Genetic algorithm optimizing 4 device parameters per patient
  • Hardware validation on custom benchtop robotic actuator
  • Fluoroscopic + camera tracking for ground truth measurement
Optimized Parameters

03

Methodology

🫀
Anatomy Benchmarking

Pulled 100 patient ICA geometries from open-source TOF-MRA data. Built a classification scheme around tortuosity and curvature severity, creating the first systematic anatomy benchmark for device testing in neuroendovascular navigation research.

⚗️
Physics-Based Simulation

SOFA Framework with BeamAdapter plugin for catheter and guidewire mechanics inside patient-specific vascular models. Full collision stack, beam mechanics, and tissue contact forces, enabling realistic prediction of device behavior before hardware.

🧬
Genetic Optimization

Evolutionary algorithm that treats tip length, diameter, Young's modulus, and shaft stiffness as genes. Fitness function balances navigation success rate, contact forces, and tip deflection, evolving device geometry toward the optimal configuration for each individual patient's ICA.

🔬
Hardware Validation

Custom benchtop robotic actuator running optimized device designs through anatomical phantoms. Fluoroscopic imaging and camera tracking provide ground truth measurements of navigation success, tip deflection, and contact forces, confirming that simulation predictions translate to real-world behavior.
